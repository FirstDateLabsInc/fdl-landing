## A. Attachment Style

### Secure

**S1**

> Within a few months, I introduce the person I’m seeing to my friends or social events.
> 

**S2**

> When they say they need support, I usually call or meet them that day.
> 

**S3**

> During conflicts, I keep talking or texting until we reach some kind of agreement.
> 

---

### Anxious

**AX1**

> When they’re out late and quiet online, I keep checking my phone and send repeated messages or calls.
> 

**AX2**

> After they go out with friends I don’t know, I ask many detailed questions about what happened.
> 

**AX3**

> In a typical month, I start serious talks because their replies feel slow or less frequent.
> 

---

### Avoidant

**AV1**

> If they message a lot or want to meet many days in a row, I slow my replies or suggest less time together.
> 

**AV2**

> When life gets stressful, I rarely update the person I’m seeing and handle things alone.
> 

**AV3**

> After a very deep conversation, I usually wait longer than usual to plan the next meetup.
> 

---

### Disorganized / Fearful

**D1**

> Some weeks I message a lot; other weeks I suddenly go quiet or cancel plans with no clear reason.
> 

**D2**

> After a date or talk where we felt very close or shared a lot, I sometimes avoid seeing them or slow my replies for the next few days, even if I liked it at the time.
> 

**D3**

> In conflicts, I may send many messages at first, then later go distant about the same issue.
>

## B. Communication Style

### Passive

**COM_PASSIVE_1**

> When I don’t like their plan for a date (time, place, activity), I often say ‘whatever you want’ instead of giving my real preference.
> 

**COM_PASSIVE_2**

> “When they cancel on me last minute, I usually reply with something like ‘no worries!’ and drop it, even if I’m annoyed.”
> 

---

### Aggressive

**COM_AGGRESSIVE_1**

> In arguments, I often start talking much louder or sending long angry messages before I can stop myself.
> 

**COM_AGGRESSIVE_2**

> I often say things like ‘you always…’ or ‘you never…’ in fights.
> 

---

### Passive-Aggressive

**COM_PAGG_1**

> If I feel ignored in a chat (they reply slowly or give half answers), I reply with very short messages like ‘ok’ or go silent.”
> 

**COM_PAGG_2** 

> if they forget something they said they would do for me (like helping with something), I say it’s okay at the time, but later I say little things like ‘you always forget’ instead of having a real talk about it.
> 

---

### Assertive

**COM_ASSERTIVE_1**

> In disagreements, I describe what happened and suggest a specific change for next time.
> 

**COM_ASSERTIVE_2**

> If they make ‘jokes’ about you that actually hurt, you tell them directly and explain how you’d like them to joke with you instead.
>

### Scenario (multiple choice)

> 
>

## D. Emotional Availability

**EA1**

> 

**EA2** *(reverse)*

> When I’m under pressure, I mostly stick to light topics and hide what’s really going on.
> 

**EA3**

> If I’m hurt by something they did, I eventually bring it up.
> 

**EA4** *(reverse)*

> When they start a serious talk (labels, future, feelings), I often delay or dodge the topic.

## E. Sexual Attitudes & Intimacy

### Intimacy Comfort

**IC1**

> After a few good dates, I’m comfortable giving a hug hello/goodbye or holding hands while we walk.
> 

**IC2**

> I ask or talk with them about what kinds of touch they like or don’t like (for example, kissing, cuddling, where they don’t want to be touched).
> 

**IC3**

> On dates, I sometimes take the lead by reaching for their hand, leaning in for a hug, or sitting closer, instead of always waiting for them.
> 

---

### Boundary Assertiveness

**BA1**

> If we are kissing or cuddling and I don’t want to go further, I say something like ‘let’s stop here’ or ‘I’m not ready to do more.
> 

**BA2**

> If one of us wants sex or physical intimacy much more often than the other, I bring it up and talk about how to handle it in a way that feels okay for both of us.
> 

**BA3** *(reverse)*

> I sometimes continue physical or sexual activity mainly to avoid conflict or disappointing them.

## F. Love Languages

 

**LL1 – Words of Affirmation (give)**

> When someone I’m seeing has a big day (like an exam, interview, or show), I often send a message saying I’m proud of them or that I believe in them.
> 

**LL2 – Words of Affirmation (receive)**

> Hearing them say things like ‘I really like you’ or ‘you did great’ makes me feel more loved than getting a gift.
> 

---

**LL3 – Quality Time (give)**

> When I like someone, I often suggest simple plans where we can really talk, like a walk, coffee, or cooking together at home.
> 

**LL4 – Quality Time (receive)**

> When the person I’m seeing is busy but still makes time to see me, even for something small like a quick dinner or walk, I feel very close to them.
> 

---

**LL5 – Acts of Service (give)**

> If the person I’m seeing is stressed or busy, When I’m at their place and see something I can help with (like tidying up, washing dishes, or helping with a small task), I often just start helping.
> 

**LL6 – Acts of Service (receive)**

> When the person I’m seeing takes care of something for me without me asking (like booking tickets, ordering food, or fixing something at my place), I feel especially cared for.
> 

---

**LL7 – Receiving / Giving Gifts (give)**

> When I travel or see something that fits their taste, I like to buy it or send it to them, even if it’s something small.
> 

**LL8 – Receiving / Giving Gifts (receive)**

> I usually keep small things from someone I’m seeing (like notes, tickets, or little gifts) for a long time instead of throwing them away quickly.
> 

---

**LL9 – Physical Touch (give)**

> When I feel comfortable with someone I’m seeing, I often move closer to them, like sitting side by side or lightly touching their arm or shoulder.
> 

**LL10 – Physical Touch (receive)**

> When I’m close with someone I’m seeing, being hugged or cuddling with them usually makes me feel more connected than just talking.
